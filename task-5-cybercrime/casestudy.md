# Case Study: Phishing Attack

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers try to steal sensitive information such as usernames, passwords, or bank details by pretending to be a trustworthy source.

In a typical phishing attack, the victim receives an email or message that appears to be from a bank or trusted website. The message often contains a link asking the user to verify their account or update details. When the user clicks the link, they are redirected to a fake website that looks real. Once the user enters their login or banking information, the attacker collects the data and uses it for fraud.

Students and young internet users are common targets because they frequently use online platforms and may not always verify links. The consequences include financial loss, identity theft, and unauthorized access to accounts.

Phishing attacks can be prevented by being cautious while clicking links, verifying website URLs, and not sharing sensitive information online.
